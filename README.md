# IOI in GPT-2 small — attribution patching of every hidden coordinate

One self-contained notebook (`ioi_full_activations.ipynb`) that applies
**attribution patching with [nnsight](https://nnsight.net)** to **every hidden
coordinate** of GPT-2 small on the **IOI** task — components, attention-head
dimensions, and MLP neurons — then:

1. **recovers** the IOI circuit of Wang et al. (2022),
2. **traces** the paper's circuit (Fig 2) annotated with the new numbers,
3. **regenerates the paper's figures** (Figs 1, 2, 3b, 3c, 4b, 5b, **6, 7, 8**) and
   the MLP appendix (**Fig 19 / Appendix J**),
4. goes **one level below components** — to neurons in the MLPs and dimensions
   inside attention heads — and redoes Figs 6 & 7 in the **neuron basis**,
5. **stores** the full coordinate-level attributions in a **Zarr** store (the
   "storage challenge").

Dataset: [`mib-bench/ioi`](https://huggingface.co/datasets/mib-bench/ioi).
Paper: *Interpretability in the Wild* (arXiv:2211.00593), in `paper/`.
Neuron-basis framing follows [transluce.org/neuron-circuits](https://transluce.org/neuron-circuits).

## Method

Denoising attribution patching with nnsight, metric = `logit(IO) − logit(S)`:
`attr = (a_clean − a_corrupt) · ∂metric_corrupt/∂a_corrupt`, computed for every
hidden unit from one clean forward + one corrupt forward + one backward.
Gradients use nnsight's `retain_grad()` + `.grad.save()` inside
`with metric.backward():`. Intermediate-node figures (4b/5b) use **edge
attribution patching**, with the gradient projected through **each layer's own
`ln_1`** (not the model's final `ln_f` reused everywhere). The
completeness/minimality/MLP figures (6/7/19) use **mean-ablation knockouts**
over the paper's p_ABC distribution, where the reference mean is computed from
samples sharing the **same template** (not just the same token length — the
paper's stated reason: grammatical role at a position varies across templates).

Where the paper specifies an exact procedure we follow it literally rather than
approximate: Fig 7's per-head `K` sets are the paper's own sets from Appendix K /
Figure 20 (not "the rest of the head's class"), and Fig 6 implements all three of
the paper's sampling strategies including the **greedy search of Algorithm 3**
with its exact selection rule (10 runs, keep the 5 highest-incompleteness sets).

## Key results (GPT-2 small, ~500 IOI examples for attribution; 85-example
single-template subset for mean-ablation, per the paper's "same template" rule)

- Clean logit diff ≈ **+3.4** (paper 3.56); ~**19/26** circuit heads recovered in the top-26.
- **Fig 3b** name movers 9.9/9.6/10.0 (+), negative name movers 10.7/11.10 (−);
  **Fig 4b** lights up S-inhibition 8.6 strongly; **Fig 5b** lights up induction/duplicate heads.
- **Fig 7 (minimality), paper's exact K-sets:** large, paper-shaped scores — negative
  name movers 10.7/11.10 ≈65%/49%, duplicate-token 0.1/0.10 ≈44%/51%, induction 5.5/6.9
  ≈49%/44%, previous-token 4.11 ≈42% (much sharper than a naive "same-class" approximation).
- **Fig 6 (completeness), all 3 sampling strategies incl. greedy (Algorithm 3):** greedy
  reaches ≈33% incompleteness, random/class ≈44% — on our (smaller-scale) run greedy does
  not clearly beat random the way the paper's 87% does; we attribute this to a handful of
  individually huge-minimality heads making random sampling "get lucky" easily at our
  scale. The qualitative pattern still replicates: greedy's points sit where the small
  circuit's own performance collapses while the full model stays intact (see figure).
- **Fig 19**: knocking out **MLP0 alone reverses the sign of the logit difference**
  (F: +2.86 → −0.52); every other MLP layer barely moves it — reproduces Appendix J.
- **Neuron basis (a real finding):** attention is **sparse in the coordinate basis**
  (top 3200/9216 attention coordinates recover 100% of F(M)) but the MLP pathway is
  **dense in the neuron basis** — no MLP-neuron subset we tried (25 to 3200 neurons)
  recovers a positive logit diff; the contribution is spread across most of the 3072
  neurons in each layer, dominated by MLP0. This nuances the "circuits are sparse in the
  neuron basis" picture (transluce.org/neuron-circuits) for IOI: attention compresses,
  the MLP pathway does not. Beyond the paper, heads **9.4, 6.0, 11.6** are robust
  non-canonical contributors not in the published circuit.
